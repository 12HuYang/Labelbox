Labelbox: February Product Update

Hello,

Thank you for using Labelbox. The month of February saw significant improvements to Labelbox, including the ability to review and edit labels. Read on to learn about new product features, data privacy updates, post beta plans and pricing.

1. Review or edit labels
Reviewing and sometimes editing the labels is an important part of data labeling. Labeled data can be reviewed and edited by going to activity tab inside the project.

2. Roles & Permissions
You can now set collaborator roles: “admin” or “labeler” and manage project level access for labelers.

3. Keyboard shortcuts
The image segmentation template now supports comprehensive keyboard shortcuts to drastically speed up the labeling process. Highly recommend checking them out!

4. File Upload
It’s now possible to directly upload data to Labelbox from your local computer in addition to importing data already hosted in the cloud by uploading a CSV file with URLs.

On data privacy

In February we updated our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy to reflect the way we handle the data you provide to Labelbox. The data you provide to Labelbox is owned by you and is only available to you and other collaborators you share your Labelbox projects with. You can read our Terms of Use, Privacy Policy, and Copyright Dispute Policy here.

We know that for some businesses, it is a requirement to keep their data inside their firewall. To this end, we have updated Labelbox such that it can be used in a way that the user’s data never leaves their private network. To learn more about how to use Labelbox in this way, read our simple guide to use Labelbox with internally hosted data. As part of the Labelbox Enterprise offering, we take this one step further by enabling client side encryption of the labeling interfaces.

Labelbox Beta ends March 19th!

We are delighted to share that Labelbox will be out of beta on March 19th, 2018.

Starting March 19th, Labelbox will come in three flavors.

  1. Labelbox Community - Free
    Ideal for small projects and evaluation of Labelbox. You can invite as many collaborators to Labelbox as you want, but the maximum number of data rows (images, text snippets, etc… ) that can be labeled is 5,000 per organization annually.

    All labels created before March 19th will not be counted against the labeled data limit of Labelbox Community.

  2. Labelbox Enterprise - Contact us
    Leverage the Labelbox Enterprise offering to significantly reduce data labeling costs by eliminating your need to build and maintain in-house machine learning infrastructure. Let us show you how Labelbox reduces your cost/label and increases labeled data quality and consistency. Contact us at support@labelbox.com to learn more.

  3. Labelbox Open Source - Contact us
    Labelbox is only possible because of open source projects and public academic work. In addition to making our labeling interface open source, we can offer a custom license of Labelbox to open source projects and public academic research, called Labelbox Open Source. Contact us at support@labelbox.com for more information.

Thanks for using Labelbox and helping us improve it throughout the beta.
